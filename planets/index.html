The Solar System

Planets

A tour from the Sun outward

1

Mercury

Closest to the Sun; small, rocky, and heavily cratered.

  • Orbital period: ~88 Earth days
  • No atmosphere to speak of
2

Venus

Similar in size to Earth; thick CO₂ atmosphere, runaway greenhouse.

  • Extremely hot surface
  • Rotates slowly in the opposite direction
3

Earth

Our home: liquid water, life, and a protective atmosphere.

  • One natural satellite: the Moon
  • Orbital period: ~365 days
4

Mars

The Red Planet; thin atmosphere, ancient riverbeds, and polar ice.

  • Two moons: Phobos and Deimos
  • Target for robotic and future human exploration
5

Jupiter

Largest planet; gas giant with a famous Great Red Spot.

  • Dozens of moons, including Europa and Ganymede
  • Mostly hydrogen and helium
6

Saturn

Gas giant with spectacular rings made of ice and rock.

  • Many moons; Titan has a thick atmosphere
  • Less dense than water
7

Uranus & Neptune

Ice giants: cold, blue, and rich in ices (water, ammonia, methane).

  • Uranus: tilted on its side
  • Neptune: strong winds and dark storms
